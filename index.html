Working Paper 2024
The Effect of Minimum Wage on Youth Employment: Evidence from Administrative Data
with Co-author Name
We study the employment effects of minimum wage increases using linked employer–employee administrative data covering 2000–2020. Exploiting variation in minimum wage levels across states and over time, we find that a 10 percent increase in the minimum wage reduces youth employment by 1.2–1.8 percentage points, with larger effects concentrated among low-skill workers and in high-exposure industries.

Working Paper 2024
Tax Incidence and Inequality: Revisiting the Evidence
sole author
This paper re-examines the distributional effects of corporate taxation using newly available matched firm-worker data. I develop a structural model allowing for heterogeneous incidence across worker and firm types, finding that the burden falls disproportionately on low-wage workers in capital-intensive industries.
